The app

Is Pocket Breath Coach free?

Yes. The free version includes seven complete guided sessions, fully adjustable breathing patterns, reminders, favorites and the sleep timer — with no time limits and no account required. Premium unlocks the full library: 260+ sessions, 50+ scenes and 60+ soundscapes.

Which breathing techniques are included?

Box breathing, 4-7-8 breathing, even 1:1 rhythms, calming 4:6 and 1:2 extended-exhale ratios, and pranayama-inspired patterns — over 260 preset variations organized into rows like For Sleep, Daytime Calm, and Focus & Clarity.

Every preset is a starting point: adjust the inhale, exhale and both holds to a tenth of a second, then save your own pattern to favorites.

Can it help me fall asleep?

Sleep is the app's strongest use case. Long-exhale sessions slow your breathing down, night scenes keep the light low, and the sleep timer gently fades the sound out and ends the session after you've drifted off. Audio keeps playing while your phone is locked, so you can put the screen down and just listen.

Does the app work offline?

Yes. Free sessions work entirely offline, and Premium subscribers can download the full sound library to their phone. Flights, waiting rooms and camping trips are exactly where the app earns its keep.

Does it work with my phone locked?

Yes — breathing cues and soundscapes keep playing in the background with the screen off or the phone locked, with media controls on your lock screen. Many people run their wind-down session entirely by ear.

What platforms are supported?

iPhone (via the App Store) and Android (via Google Play).

Pricing & subscriptions

How much does Premium cost?

$0.99 per month or $6.99 per year (US pricing; local prices vary by region). The yearly plan saves 41% versus paying monthly. For comparison, Calm and Headspace are $69.99 per year — about ten times as much.

How do I cancel my subscription?

Subscriptions are billed and managed by Apple or Google, not by us — so cancelling is exactly like any other subscription on your phone:

On iPhone: Settings → your name → Subscriptions → Pocket Breath Coach → Cancel.
On Android: Play Store → profile icon → Payments & subscriptions → Subscriptions.

Premium stays active until the end of the period you've already paid for.

I got a new phone. How do I restore Premium?

Install the app, open Settings inside the app and tap Restore Purchase. As long as you're signed in to the same Apple ID or Google account you subscribed with, Premium comes right back — nothing to re-buy.

Is there a free trial of Premium?

The free tier is the trial — seven full sessions with no expiry, so you can practice for as long as you like before deciding. At $6.99 a year, Premium is priced to be an easy yes rather than something you need to test-drive under a deadline.

Accounts & privacy

Do I need an account?

No. The app works fully without one. An optional account (email and password) syncs your favorites and reminders across devices — that's all it's for.

What data do you collect?

As little as we can get away with. No ads, no third-party trackers, and no analytics SDKs — we deliberately removed them. Your favorites and session history stay on your device unless you sign in to sync them. Anonymous crash reports are the only diagnostics we receive. The full details are in the privacy policy.

How do I delete my account and data?

In the app: Settings → Delete Account. This permanently removes your account and all synced data. If you no longer have the app installed, follow the steps at pocketbreathcoach.com/delete-account.

The practice

Why slow breathing?

Slow, extended-exhale breathing is one of the most direct ways to activate the parasympathetic nervous system — the body's rest-and-digest response. It's the mechanism behind box breathing used by military and first responders, the 4-7-8 pattern popularized for sleep, and the paced breathing taught in clinical settings. The app takes care of the pacing so you can just follow along.

How long should a session be?

A few minutes is genuinely enough to feel a shift — most people settle in somewhere between 3 and 10 minutes. For sleep, start the session when you get into bed and let the sleep timer end it for you.

Is slow breathing safe for everyone?

Slow, comfortable breathing is gentle for most people. That said, Pocket Breath Coach is not a medical device and isn't a substitute for professional care. If you're pregnant, have a respiratory or cardiovascular condition, or ever feel dizzy or short of breath during practice, return to normal breathing and check with your doctor. Never use the app while driving.
